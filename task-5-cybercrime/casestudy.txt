This case study is about phishing, a common type of cybercrime. Phishing is a method used by cybercriminals to trick people into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data.

In a typical phishing attack, the attacker sends a fake email or message that appears to be from a trusted source, such as a bank or a popular website. The message usually contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website that looks real. When the user enters their details, the attacker captures the information.

Students and young internet users are often targeted because they may not always verify the authenticity of such messages. For example, a student might receive an email claiming that their account needs urgent verification and click on the link without checking the sender.

The consequences of phishing can be serious. Victims may lose money, personal data, or access to their accounts. It can also lead to identity theft and misuse of personal information.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting online and always verifying the source before sharing any sensitive information.
